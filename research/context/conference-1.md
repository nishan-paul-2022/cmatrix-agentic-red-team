## 5. Strategic Submission Guidelines

### The AoE Deadline Rule
All deadlines above are listed in **Anytime on Earth (AoE)** time. Because Bangladesh is UTC+6, an AoE deadline technically ends at **6:00 PM the following calendar day** in local Dhaka time. 
* *Example:* A deadline of Aug 19 AoE is actually Aug 20 at 6:00 PM in Bangladesh. Always verify against an official countdown clock.

### Double-Blind Rigor
Top-tier venues like USENIX Security and ACM CCS have strict anonymity requirements. 
* Do not include author names, affiliations, or funding acknowledgments in the submission draft.
* Cite your own prior work in the third person (e.g., "In [X], Smith et al. showed..." rather than "In our previous work...").
* Ensure any repositories linked are fully anonymized.

### Open Science & Artifacts
"Casual" links to personal repositories are insufficient for these venues.
* **Open Science Appendix:** Most venues now require an appendix detailing the artifacts used.
* **Anonymity:** Artifacts must be hosted on anonymous platforms during the review phase.
* **Availability:** USENIX Security 2026, for example, expects artifacts to be available at the time of submission or justified if they are not. Accepted papers must keep artifacts publicly available.

### Participation & Visas
While hybrid options existed during the pandemic, many venues are returning to an in-person priority.
* **Format:** Some venues, like CCS 2027, are explicitly planning for in-person formats.
* **Remote Accommodations:** Do not assume remote presentation is always an option. Check the specific registration guidelines for "Virtual Pass" options (like ICLR 2026) early in the submission process.
