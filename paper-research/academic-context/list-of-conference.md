### Cost Management

- **Bangladesh qualifies** for GNI-based free virtual registration at IEEE VIS and similar venues
- **PETS** offers free virtual attendance for developing countries — apply explicitly
- **NDSS Fellowship** (Internet Society) — 24 funded per year. Apply as soon as paper is accepted
- **USENIX student grants** — email `student-grants@usenix.org` after acceptance
- **NeurIPS/AAAI D&I subsidies** — deadline usually 6–8 weeks before conference
- Most virtual presenter fees: $100–175, within the $200 budget

### Paper Angles for CMatrix

| Paper Type | Best Target Venues |
|---|---|
| Survey: agentic AI pentesting landscape | NDSS, S&P, AsiaCCS |
| LangGraph automated vuln discovery + evaluation | USENIX Security, ACSAC |
| ML-based exploit/attack generation | NeurIPS, ICML, AAAI |
| Privacy/security of AI agents | PETS, CODASPY |
| Network scan automation | IEEE CNS, CANS, ACNS |

### Important Reminders

1. **At least one author must register** at every venue — no exceptions or the paper is removed
2. **Open Science policy** at USENIX 2026+ requires sharing code/artifacts at submission — CMatrix being open-source is an advantage
3. **Workshop papers** are real citable publications — don't underestimate them for PhD apps
4. **arXiv preprint** — post simultaneously with any submission to establish priority and visibility
5. **UC Berkeley, CMU, MIT, Stanford** all look at top-4 security (S&P, CCS, USENIX, NDSS) and top AI (NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR) publications most heavily
